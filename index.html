A New Dawn for Your Child’s Education

Welcome to Lumina Terra: the Light of the Earth – where every child’s light shines brighter.

Lumina Terra School of Nations is not just a school—it’s a vibrant, sustainable, and globally inspired learning community where your child’s journey is rooted in the earth and reaches for the stars. Here, every child is seen, heard, and valued, and every day is an invitation to wonder, create, and grow.

Stages, not Ages

Currently welcoming children ages 2‐10, Lumina Terra will expand into a full CBSE K‐12 school with an option for the IB Diploma Programme — guided by an international board with experience from Norway, France, Finland, the USA, and India.

“Every leaf holds a lesson, every breeze tells a story—our classrooms are alive.”

A Rounded Education

At Lumina Terra, we believe every child is a light for the world.

Our unique Rounded Education nurtures each child’s mind, body, spirit, and heart — cultivating both Inner Powers (intellect, thought, imagination, memory, comprehension) and Outer Powers (the five senses) in a balanced, joyful way.

  • Competency-based, mastery learning ‐ two grades ahead, naturally
  • Small classes, big attention ‐ individual focus in a warm community
  • Lighter bags ‐ most materials kept at school for ease
  • Daily development of new skills and talents ‐ embedded in the curriculum
  • Kindness, empathy, and honesty ‐ woven into daily life
  • Rooted in tradition ‐ integrates the latest advancements
  • Prepares children better ‐ measured by external evaluations

Circular Learning Spaces

With no beginning or end, our circular structures — Yurts by Lee Jingyi, Zome by Michael Feneux, and natural playgrounds by Olivier Ledoux nurture balance, calm, and a deep sense of oneness with self and spirit.

Dynamic learning spaces are carefully designed and organized — inspiring calm focus, joyful exploration, and true collaboration.

Immersive Skill Development

Children build creativity, innovation, leadership, resilience, and real-world problem-solving through working in pairs and peer groups with focus on:

  • Nature & sustainability
  • Creative arts & crafts
  • Science & technology
  • Entrepreneurship & global citizenship experiences
